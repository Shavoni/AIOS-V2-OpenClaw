# ATLAS Revenue Modules

**Version:** 1.0
**Last Updated:** 2026-05-06
**Scope:** Covers Research Domain 15 (Ancillary revenue modules including Merch, VIP Upgrades, Sync Licensing, and Field Ops).

## Executive Summary
For independent venues and artists, ticketing alone is rarely enough to sustain profitability. The ATLAS platform is designed as a "Revenue Stack," integrating multiple monetization streams directly into the fan's digital journey. This document outlines the core ancillary revenue modules: the digital merch marketplace, dynamic VIP upgrades, and the experimental integration of live performance sync licensing.

---

## Part A — The Merch Engine & "Merch Cuts"

The practice of venues taking a "merch cut" (often 15% - 25% of an artist's gross merchandise sales) has become a massive point of contention in the live music industry. ATLAS re-engineers this dynamic.

### 1. The Digital Merch Booth
*   **The Problem:** Long lines at physical merch tables lead to abandoned sales, and cash handling increases shrinkage.
*   **The ATLAS Solution:** The platform integrates a digital merch store directly into the ticket wallet. Fans can purchase "Click & Collect" merch 48 hours before the show or scan a QR code at their table to order during the event.
*   **The Compromise Cut:** Because ATLAS handles the payment processing and inventory management (relieving the venue of staffing the physical booth), the platform enforces a capped, standardized "Digital Merch Fee" of **7%** (split 5% to the Venue, 2% to ATLAS), leaving 93% for the artist.

### 2. Post-Show Print-on-Demand (POD)
*   If an artist sells out of physical stock on tour, the ATLAS app automatically flips the item to a Print-on-Demand (POD) model via API integration with partners like Printful, capturing revenue that would otherwise be lost.

## Part B — VIP Upgrades & Bottle Service

In many global markets (particularly Africa and LatAm), VIP table sales and bottle service constitute over 60% of a venue's gross revenue.

### 1. Dynamic VIP Upgrades
*   **Pre-Show Upsells:** 24 hours before the event, the Concierge AI identifies unsold VIP inventory (e.g., a 6-person booth). It sends an automated push notification to high-value GA ticket holders offering a discounted upgrade.
*   **In-Venue Ordering:** The ATLAS ticket wallet includes a localized menu for the venue. Fans can order bottle service directly to their table using their stored credit card or mobile money wallet, bypassing the waitstaff bottleneck.

### 2. "Skip the Line" & Fast Track
*   A high-margin, zero-cost digital product. The platform allows venues to sell a limited number of "Fast Track" QR codes, granting expedited entry through a dedicated security lane managed by the Field Ops App.

## Part C — Live Sync Licensing & Data Monetization

This is ATLAS's most experimental and potentially lucrative module, bridging the gap between live performance and digital rights.

### 1. The Live Recording Sync Engine
*   **Concept:** Many independent venues have high-quality soundboards capable of capturing multi-track live recordings.
*   **The Flow:** 
    1.  The ATLAS Performance Agreement (generated via the Compliance Engine) includes an optional "Live Recording & Sync Opt-In" clause.
    2.  If the artist agrees, the venue captures the live audio.
    3.  The audio is uploaded to the ATLAS Sync Library (integrated with B2B sync agencies like Synchtank or Songtradr).
    4.  If the live track is placed in a film, TV show, or commercial, the sync fee is automatically split (e.g., 70% Artist, 15% Venue, 15% ATLAS) via the platform's smart ledger.

### 2. Zero-Party Data Monetization
*   By acting as the centralized CRM for hundreds of independent venues, ATLAS aggregates highly valuable "Zero-Party Data" (data fans explicitly choose to share, such as genre preferences and beverage choices).
*   **Sponsorship Dashboards:** Venues can use this anonymized data to negotiate better sponsorships. *Example:* A venue can prove to Red Bull that 40% of its Afrobeats attendees purchase energy drinks, justifying a higher brand activation fee.
