# ATLAS Onboarding Playbooks (Venue, Promoter, Artist)

**Version:** 1.0
**Last Updated:** 2026-05-06
**Scope:** Covers Research Domain 17 (SaaS adoption, B2B onboarding, and user training for the ATLAS platform).

## Executive Summary
A SaaS platform is only as valuable as its adoption rate. The live music industry is notoriously resistant to new technology, often preferring legacy spreadsheets and WhatsApp groups. This document outlines the standardized onboarding playbooks designed to reduce friction, ensure compliance, and drive immediate "Time-to-Value" (TTV) for Venues, Promoters, and Artists joining the ATLAS network.

---

## Part A — The Venue Onboarding Playbook

Venues are the physical infrastructure of the ATLAS network. Onboarding must be rigorous for compliance, but seamless for the operator.

### 1. The 72-Hour "Go-Live" Sequence
*   **Day 1: KYC & Digital Twin Creation.**
    *   Venue owner uploads corporate registry docs and IDs for automated KYC/AML clearing.
    *   The venue uploads a PDF of their floor plan. The ATLAS Design Team (or automated AI tool) converts this into a digital, interactive seat map for the ticketing engine.
*   **Day 2: Financial Integration & Compliance.**
    *   Venue connects their local bank account or mobile money wallet via Flutterwave/Stripe.
    *   Venue uploads PRO certificates (e.g., PRS, SAMRO) and Insurance COIs.
*   **Day 3: Field Ops Training.**
    *   The venue's head of security and box office manager complete a mandatory 30-minute interactive module on using the ATLAS Field Ops App (offline scanning, fast-track routing, and VIP table management).

### 2. Hardware Deployment (Optional)
*   For venues in emerging markets lacking modern POS systems, ATLAS deploys a "Box Office in a Box" kit: 2x ruggedized Android tablets pre-loaded with the Field Ops App and 2x Bluetooth receipt printers.

## Part B — The Promoter Onboarding Playbook

Promoters are the liquidity providers of the network; they bring the talent and the risk capital.

### 1. The "First Offer" Milestone
*   The primary goal of promoter onboarding is to get them to generate their first automated Performance Agreement via the Concierge AI.
*   **The Sandbox Environment:** New promoters are placed in a "Sandbox Mode" where they can use the Concierge to model tour routings, calculate flight costs, and simulate break-even ticket sales without committing actual funds.
*   **Escrow Education:** Promoters must complete a module explaining the ATLAS Smart Escrow system—how artist deposits are locked in USDC and only released upon cryptographic signature of the contract, protecting them from "no-show" fraud.

### 2. Marketing Stack Integration
*   Promoters are trained on integrating the ATLAS tracking pixel with their Meta Business Manager and TikTok Ads accounts, allowing them to track Return on Ad Spend (ROAS) directly within the ATLAS dashboard.

## Part C — The Artist (and Management) Onboarding Playbook

Artists (and their managers/agents) need to see ATLAS not as another ticketing app, but as a tool that guarantees they get paid accurately and on time.

### 1. The Electronic Press Kit (EPK) & Rider Integration
*   **Profile Claiming:** Artists claim their verified profile, which serves as a centralized EPK for ATLAS network promoters.
*   **The Smart Rider:** Instead of uploading a static PDF, the artist's tour manager inputs their technical and hospitality requirements into the "Smart Rider" builder. This allows the Concierge AI to automatically cross-reference the artist's needs against a venue's technical specs during the booking phase.

### 2. Financial Sovereignty Training
*   **Wallet Setup:** Artists are guided through setting up their payout preferences, choosing between domestic fiat wire transfers or direct USDC stablecoin payouts to a non-custodial wallet (critical for artists touring from regions with high FX volatility or capital controls).
*   **Tax Transparency:** A brief module explaining how the ATLAS Settlement Agent automatically calculates and escrows foreign withholding taxes, ensuring the artist is never hit with a surprise tax bill at the end of a tour.
