# ATLAS 18-Month V2 Roadmap & Strategic Options

**Version:** 1.0
**Last Updated:** 2026-05-06
**Scope:** Covers Research Domain 20 (Post-MVP roadmap, AI autonomy expansion, secondary market strategy, and M&A potential).

## Executive Summary
Following the successful deployment of the 6-month MVP (which establishes the core ticketing and settlement infrastructure), the ATLAS platform will shift from a "Product-Led Growth" (PLG) phase into an "AI-Led Expansion" phase. This document outlines the 18-month roadmap for V2 features, specifically the deployment of the Autonomous Routing Engine and the strategic entry into the secondary ticketing market.

---

## Part A — The V2 Feature Expansion (Months 7-18)

### 1. The Autonomous AI Routing Engine (Months 7-12)
*   **The Shift:** In V1, the Concierge AI acted as an assistant (RAG-based contract generation). In V2, it becomes an active orchestrator.
*   **The Feature:** Promoters can input a budget (e.g., "$50,000") and a region (e.g., "Scandinavia"). The AI will autonomously calculate the Traveling Salesman Problem (TSP) using live Amadeus flight APIs, cross-reference visa rules, and output three fully costed tour routing options.
*   **Agentic Booking:** The AI will draft the offers and place them in the promoter's outbox, requiring only a single "Approve All" click to dispatch to artist agents.

### 2. The "Face-Value" Secondary Market (Months 13-18)
*   **The Strategic Move:** Secondary ticketing (resale) is highly lucrative but ethically fraught.
*   **The ATLAS Model:** V2 introduces a closed-loop, fan-to-fan exchange.
*   **The Mechanics:** Fans can only resell tickets within the ATLAS app. The price is hard-capped at the original purchase price plus a 5% platform transfer fee. This eliminates scalper arbitrage while providing liquidity to fans and capturing the secondary transaction fee for the platform.

## Part B — Data Monetization & Sync Licensing

### 1. The Live Sync API Integration
*   As outlined in the Revenue Modules, V2 will integrate the ATLAS backend with B2B sync agencies (e.g., Synchtank).
*   The platform will automate the ingest of live audio stems from the venue's soundboard, tag them with metadata (Artist, Venue, Date), and push them to the sync library, creating a passive revenue stream for both the venue and the artist.

### 2. Predictive "Zero-Party" Data Dashboards
*   By Month 18, ATLAS will have aggregated millions of data points across its independent venue network.
*   V2 will launch a premium B2B dashboard for global brands (e.g., Diageo, Red Bull), allowing them to buy highly targeted experiential marketing activations based on real-time fan demographics.

## Part C — Strategic Options (M&A and Exit Scenarios)

By the end of the 18-month roadmap, ATLAS will control the ticketing, settlement, and data for a significant cohort of global independent venues. This creates several strategic options.

### 1. The "Aggregator" Acquisition Play
*   Major conglomerates (Live Nation, AEG, or tech giants like Spotify) struggle to penetrate the grassroots independent venue market because their software is too bloated and their terms are too predatory.
*   ATLAS becomes a prime acquisition target because it has successfully aggregated this highly fragmented, high-value cultural sector into a single, clean API and data lake.

### 2. The Decentralized Protocol Spin-Off
*   Alternatively, ATLAS could spin off its Settlement Ledger into a decentralized, open-source protocol (e.g., on an L2 blockchain), becoming the standard financial rail for the entire live music industry, charging a micro-transaction fee on every ticket sold globally, regardless of the consumer-facing app.
