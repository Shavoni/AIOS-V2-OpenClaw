# The Buyer Landscape: Funds, Majors, and Boutiques

## Overview

This document maps the taxonomy of entities actively acquiring music catalogs in 2026. Understanding the buyer's business model is essential for structuring a sale or a post-recovery administration deal.

## Key Concepts

Not all buyers are the same. Some are pure financial yield plays (treating songs like bonds), while others are strategic activators who buy catalogs to aggressively market and brand them.

## Detailed Information

### Taxonomy of Buyers

1.  **Strategic Activators (The Primary Wave Model):** 
    *   *Examples:* Primary Wave, Iconic Artists Group (Irving Azoff).
    *   *Strategy:* Brand-forward, marketing-heavy. They often buy partial equity stakes and invest heavily in brand building (biopics, brand partnerships) to drive up the value of the remaining copyright. They treat catalogs as dynamic brands.
2.  **Public Songs Funds & PE-Backed Plays:**
    *   *Examples:* Hipgnosis Songs Capital (Blackstone), Round Hill, KKR/Chord Music Partners.
    *   *Strategy:* Yield-focused. They acquire long-duration cash flows to provide dividend-style payouts to limited partners. Highly disciplined pricing based on DCF models.
3.  **Strategic Music Companies & Majors:**
    *   *Examples:* Concord, BMG, Sony Music Publishing, Universal, Warner Chappell.
    *   *Strategy:* Bolt-on acquisitions. They leverage their massive existing global administration infrastructure to increase the margin on acquired catalogs. BMG, for example, spent €243M on catalog acquisitions in 2024.
4.  **Boutique/Niche Acquirers:**
    *   *Examples:* Reservoir Media.
    *   *Strategy:* Curated catalogs with strong cultural resonance. Reservoir focuses on the $40M-$60M 'sweet spot' and recently acquired the Miles Davis catalog ahead of his 2026 centennial.

## Practical Applications

If a client recovers a culturally significant legacy catalog (e.g., Norman Whitfield), the agent will recommend pitching to Strategic Activators like Primary Wave or Reservoir Media, as these buyers have the infrastructure to revive dormant brands, rather than selling to a pure financial yield fund.

## Escalation Requirements

Escalate to an investment banker or specialized music broker when a client is ready to solicit formal bids, as the agent cannot conduct the auction process or negotiate binding term sheets.
