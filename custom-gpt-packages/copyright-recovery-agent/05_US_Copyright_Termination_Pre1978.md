# US Copyright Termination: Pre-1978 Works

## Overview

This document details the mechanics of terminating copyright transfers for works where the copyright was originally secured prior to January 1, 1978, governed by 17 U.S.C. § 304(c) and § 304(d).

## Key Concepts

The 1909 Copyright Act provided a 28-year initial term and a 28-year renewal term. The 1976 Act extended the renewal term, and granted authors (or their statutory heirs) the right to recapture the extended years by terminating prior grants. This is highly relevant for legacy catalogs like the Norman Whitfield Motown catalog.

## Detailed Information

### 17 U.S.C. § 304(c) Mechanics
*   **Applicability:** Applies to any copyright subsisting in its first or renewal term on January 1, 1978.
*   **The Window:** The termination window opens **56 years** from the date the copyright was originally secured (either by publication with notice or registration as an unpublished work).
*   **Duration of Window:** The window remains open for a period of **five years**.
*   **Notice Requirement:** A Notice of Termination must be served on the grantee no less than **2 years** and no more than **10 years** before the effective date of termination.

### 17 U.S.C. § 304(d) Fallback
*   **Applicability:** Provides a fallback termination right for authors who missed their § 304(c) window.
*   **The Window:** Opens **75 years** after the copyright was secured.
*   **Condition:** This right *only* exists if the termination right under § 304(c) was never previously exercised and the copyright was still in its renewal term on October 26, 1998 (the effective date of the Sonny Bono Copyright Term Extension Act).

### Who Can Exercise
The author. If the author is deceased, the right vests in the surviving spouse and children/grandchildren, requiring a majority of the termination interest to act.

## Practical Applications

For a song registered in 1966 (e.g., 'Double Shot (Of My Baby's Love)'), the 56-year mark was 2022. The 5-year window closes in 2027. The agent must calculate these dates precisely and flag that notice must be served at least 2 years prior to the chosen effective date within that window.

## Escalation Requirements

Escalate immediately if the agent calculates that a § 304(c) or § 304(d) 5-year window is closing within the next 24 months, as the required 2-year advance notice period may already be exhausted, requiring emergency legal triage.
