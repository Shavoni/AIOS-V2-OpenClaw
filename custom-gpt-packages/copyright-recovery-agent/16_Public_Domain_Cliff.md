# The Public Domain Cliff

## Overview

This document explains the mechanics of copyright expiration and the rolling entry of works into the public domain. Understanding the PD cliff is essential for assessing the long-term value of older catalogs.

## Key Concepts

Copyright protection is not infinite. For works created before 1978, the maximum term (if properly renewed) is 95 years from publication. Once a work enters the public domain, it loses its exclusive commercial value.

## Detailed Information

### The 95-Year Rule
For works published or registered before January 1, 1978, the maximum copyright term is 95 years from the date of publication (an initial 28-year term plus a 67-year renewal term). 

### The Rolling Public Domain
Every January 1st ('Public Domain Day'), a new year of works loses copyright protection. 
*   **In 2024:** Works published in 1928 entered the PD.
*   **In 2025:** Works published in 1929 entered the PD.
*   **In 2026:** Works published in 1930 entered the PD.

### Strategic Implications
*   **Valuation:** A catalog heavily weighted with works from the 1930s has a rapidly depreciating value. A buyer will heavily discount the multiple because the cash flow will drop to zero when the works hit the PD cliff.
*   **Derivative Works:** Before a work enters the PD, an estate might strategically authorize derivative works (like a new musical arrangement or a film adaptation) to create new copyrighted assets based on the original.

## Practical Applications

If asked to evaluate a catalog of jazz standards published in 1935, the agent will immediately flag that the copyrights will expire in 2030 (95 years later). It will advise that any termination action must account for the fact that the recovered rights will only have a 4-year commercial lifespan.

## Escalation Requirements

Escalate to counsel if there is ambiguity about the original publication date or whether the required renewal filings were made during the 28th year (for works pre-1964), as failure to renew may mean the work is already in the public domain.
