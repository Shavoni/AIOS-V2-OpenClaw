# Glossary and Definitions

## Overview

This document defines the specialized legal and financial terminology used in copyright termination and music publishing. It ensures the agent and the user operate with a shared vocabulary.

## Key Concepts

Precision in language is critical. Confusing a 'composition' with a 'sound recording', or 'execution date' with 'publication date', can result in fatal errors in termination filings.

## Detailed Information

### Legal Terms
*   **Composition (Musical Work):** The underlying music and lyrics (the song itself). Usually controlled by a music publisher.
*   **Sound Recording (Master):** The specific audio fixation of a performance of a composition. Usually controlled by a record label.
*   **Grant:** A transfer of copyright ownership or an exclusive license (e.g., a publishing agreement).
*   **Execution Date:** The date a contract or grant was signed.
*   **Effective Date of Termination:** The specific date (chosen by the author within the 5-year statutory window) when the rights revert back to the author.
*   **Work Made for Hire (WFH):** A work created by an employee or specially commissioned under a written agreement, where the employer is deemed the author. Exempt from termination.
*   **Derivative Work:** A new work based upon one or more preexisting works (e.g., a film adaptation, a remix). 

### Financial Terms
*   **Net Publisher's Share (NPS):** Gross royalty income minus the writer's share. The core metric for valuing a publishing catalog.
*   **Multiple:** The number applied to the annual NPS to determine the total catalog purchase price (e.g., a 15x multiple on $100k NPS = $1.5M valuation).
*   **Mechanical Royalty:** Royalties paid for the reproduction and distribution of a composition (e.g., streaming, CDs, vinyl).
*   **Performance Royalty:** Royalties paid for the public broadcast of a composition (e.g., radio, live venues, streaming platforms), collected by PROs.
*   **Synchronization (Sync) License:** A license to use music in timed relation with visual media (film, TV, video games).

## Practical Applications

The agent will use these definitions to correct users. If a user says, 'I want to terminate the copyright on my master recordings under my publishing deal,' the agent will clarify the distinction between the composition (publishing) and the sound recording (master), and analyze both separately.

## Escalation Requirements

Escalate to human counsel if there is a fundamental disagreement between the user and a third party over the definition of a contract term (e.g., what constitutes 'commercial exploitation' under a specific reversion clause).
