# Estate Activation Playbooks

## Overview

This document details case studies of successful posthumous estate activations. The core philosophy is that recovering a copyright is only step one; activating the catalog as a brand is where compound value is generated.

## Key Concepts

The most valuable estates do not rely solely on passive streaming royalties. They actively manage the artist's likeness, name, and music across film, fashion, merchandise, and brand partnerships.

## Detailed Information

### The Michael Jackson Estate (Branca/McClain Model)
*   **Status:** The gold standard of estate turnaround. From massive debt at the time of death to over $2 Billion in gross earnings since 2009.
*   **Playbook:** High-leverage partnerships (Cirque du Soleil 'ONE' show), strategic sync licensing, biopic development ('Michael'), Broadway musicals, and aggressive defense of intellectual property.

### The Miles Davis Estate
*   **Status:** Culturally elevated jazz icon managed for brand prestige.
*   **Playbook:** The 2026 Centennial Campaign. Partnerships with high-end brands (Lexus, Macintosh), fashion collaborations, and a focus on positioning Davis as a pop-culture and style icon, not just a jazz musician. Recently partnered with Reservoir Media.

### The Bob Marley Estate
*   **Status:** A masterclass in lifestyle brand extension.
*   **Playbook:** House of Marley (audio products), Marley Natural (cannabis line in partnership with Tilray), the 'One Love' biopic. The estate generates tens of millions annually by extending the Marley ethos into consumer goods.

### The Whitney Houston Estate (Primary Wave Partnership)
*   **Status:** Quadrupled earnings in the decade following her death.
*   **Playbook:** Partnered with Primary Wave (50% stake) in 2019. Executed a hologram tour, released a highly successful biopic ('I Wanna Dance with Somebody'), and secured major brand campaigns, driving a massive uplift in sync and streaming revenue.

## Practical Applications

When building an activation roadmap for a recovered catalog, the agent will draw from these playbooks. For a 1970s soul catalog, it might recommend a 36-month plan including: Month 1-12 (Audit and Re-registration), Month 12-24 (Aggressive Sync Pitching to period-piece film/TV), Month 24-36 (Documentary development and apparel licensing).

## Escalation Requirements

Escalate to branding agencies or specialized estate management firms when moving from the strategic planning phase to the execution of complex licensing or merchandising deals.
