# The Heir Problem and the Diagnostic Solution

## Overview

This document addresses the primary friction point in catalog recovery: the knowledge gap between what heirs legally own and what they understand. It defines how the agent bridges this gap.

## Key Concepts

Heirs often lack the passion, industry knowledge, and financial resources to manage legacy catalogs. They do not know what they own, what it is worth, or the deadlines they face. High-end legal counsel is often economically unviable for mid-tier catalogs.

## Detailed Information

### The Core Friction
*   **Ignorance of Rights:** Many heirs are unaware of the § 203 or § 304 termination provisions.
*   **Ignorance of Deadlines:** The 5-year termination window is a strict cliff. Once it passes, the right is gone forever. Heirs rarely track the original grant dates of their parents' works.
*   **The Cost Barrier:** Traditional entertainment attorneys charge $800–$1,500/hour. A comprehensive catalog audit and termination filing can cost tens of thousands of dollars, making recovery economically unviable for a catalog generating $30,000/year.

### The Agent's Solution
The agent democratizes access to forensic catalog analysis. By automating the extraction of registration data, the computation of termination windows, and the generation of draft notices, the agent reduces the cost of discovery to near zero.

### The Diagnostic Workflow
1.  **Intake:** Accept songwriter name or specific song titles.
2.  **Forensics:** Pull USCO and PRO data.
3.  **Computation:** Calculate the exact open and close dates for termination windows.
4.  **Valuation:** Apply current multiples to available royalty data.
5.  **Reporting:** Present a plain-language diagnostic report highlighting urgent deadlines and potential financial uplift.

## Practical Applications

When interacting with an heir, the agent must assume zero prior industry knowledge. It will not ask, 'Do you want to exercise your § 203 rights?' Instead, it will state, 'Your father signed a contract in 1991. By law, you have the right to cancel that contract and take back ownership starting in 2026. If you do nothing by 2031, you lose this right forever.'

## Escalation Requirements

Escalate to a human advisor if the heir's situation involves intra-family disputes (e.g., siblings fighting over control of the estate), as the agent cannot mediate legal conflicts among co-owners.
