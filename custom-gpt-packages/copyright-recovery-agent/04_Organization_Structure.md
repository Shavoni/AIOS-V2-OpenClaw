# Organization Structure & Ecosystem Map

## Overview

This document maps the organizational structure of the music rights ecosystem that the agent navigates. It defines the key players, institutions, and databases required for catalog forensics and recovery.

## Key Concepts

Successful copyright recovery requires interacting with a complex web of government offices, performance rights organizations (PROs), mechanical licensing collectives, and music publishers. The agent must understand the role of each entity in the chain of title and royalty flow.

## Detailed Information

### Government and Regulatory Bodies
*   **U.S. Copyright Office (USCO):** The central hub for registration and recordation. The agent relies on the USCO Public Catalog for registration dates (which start the termination clock) and recordation records (to track chain of title and file Notices of Termination).

### Royalty Collection Societies
*   **Performance Rights Organizations (PROs):** ASCAP, BMI, SESAC, and GMR in the US. The agent uses the joint ASCAP/BMI Songview database to cross-reference writer and publisher shares.
*   **The Mechanical Licensing Collective (The MLC):** Administers blanket mechanical licenses for DSPs in the US. Crucial for identifying current mechanical royalty flows and updating ownership data post-termination.
*   **Foreign Societies:** PRS (UK), GEMA (Germany), SACEM (France), JASRAC (Japan). Relevant for foreign reversion strategies.

### Industry Counterparts
*   **Major Publishers:** Sony Music Publishing, Universal Music Publishing Group (UMPG), Warner Chappell. Often the current grantees facing termination.
*   **Independent & Strategic Publishers:** Primary Wave, Concord, Reservoir Media, BMG. These entities are both competitors (in acquisition) and potential partners for administration post-recovery.

## Practical Applications

When conducting catalog forensics, the agent will systematically cross-reference the USCO Public Catalog for the original grant date, the Songview database for current performance splits, and The MLC portal for mechanical administration rights, creating a unified map of the asset's current state.

## Escalation Requirements

Escalate to human intervention if the databases show conflicting ownership records that cannot be resolved through standard documentation, or if a PRO or The MLC refuses to update their records following a valid, recorded termination.
