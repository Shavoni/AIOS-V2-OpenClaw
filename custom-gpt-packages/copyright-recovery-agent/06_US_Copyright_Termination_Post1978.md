# US Copyright Termination: Post-1978 Works

## Overview

This document covers the termination of transfers for grants executed on or after January 1, 1978, governed by 17 U.S.C. § 203. This is the most active area of termination law today, affecting the massive cohort of 1980s and 1990s catalogs.

## Key Concepts

Unlike pre-1978 works (where the clock starts on the date copyright is secured), the § 203 termination clock starts on the **date of the grant** (the execution of the contract).

## Detailed Information

### 17 U.S.C. § 203 Mechanics
*   **Applicability:** Applies to grants of transfer or licenses of copyright executed by the author on or after January 1, 1978.
*   **The Window:** The termination window opens **35 years** after the date of execution of the grant. 
    *   *Exception:* If the grant covered the right of publication, the window opens 35 years after the date of publication, or 40 years after the date of execution of the grant, whichever is earlier.
*   **Duration of Window:** The author has a **five-year** window to effectuate the termination.
*   **Notice Requirement:** Advance notice in writing must be served on the grantee no less than **2 years** and no more than **10 years** before the effective date of termination.
*   **Recordation:** The notice must be recorded with the Copyright Office before the effective date of termination.

### Key Nuances
*   **Inalienability:** Termination rights cannot be waived or contracted away in advance. Agreements stating 'in perpetuity' are still subject to termination.
*   **Gap Grants:** Grants made prior to 1978 for works created on or after January 1, 1978, present complex legal issues regarding whether § 203 or § 304 applies.

## Practical Applications

When analyzing a catalog from the 1990s, the agent will look for the original publishing agreement execution date. For a grant signed in 1991, the 35-year window opens in 2026. The agent will generate a timeline showing that notice can be served immediately for an effective date in 2028.

## Escalation Requirements

Escalate if the user cannot locate the original executed contract, as the exact date of the grant is required to compute the § 203 window. 'Unsigned contracts are not enforceable contracts,' and forensic discovery will be needed.
