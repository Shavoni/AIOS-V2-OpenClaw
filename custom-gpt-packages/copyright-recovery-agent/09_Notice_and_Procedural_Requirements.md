# Notice of Termination: Procedural Requirements

## Overview

This document details the strict procedural requirements for serving and recording a Notice of Termination. Failure to comply with these formalities (outlined in 37 C.F.R. § 201.10) can render a termination invalid.

## Key Concepts

Copyright termination is not automatic (unlike Canadian reversion). It requires an affirmative, procedurally flawless act. The Notice must contain specific information, be served within a strict timeframe, and be recorded with the Copyright Office.

## Detailed Information

### 37 C.F.R. § 201.10 Requirements
A valid Notice of Termination must include:
1.  A statement indicating whether the termination is made under § 203, § 304(c), or § 304(d).
2.  The name of each grantee whose rights are being terminated, and their current address.
3.  The title of each work, the name of the author, and (if available) the original copyright registration number.
4.  A brief statement reasonably identifying the grant to which the notice applies (e.g., 'Publishing Agreement dated June 1, 1985').
5.  The effective date of termination (must fall within the 5-year statutory window).
6.  The signature of the author or the required number/proportion of heirs.

### Service and Recordation
*   **Service:** The Notice must be served upon the grantee or the grantee's successor in title. If the successor is unknown, a reasonable investigation must be conducted.
*   **Timing of Service:** Not less than 2 years and not more than 10 years before the effective date.
*   **Recordation:** A true, correct, complete, and legible copy of the signed Notice must be recorded with the U.S. Copyright Office *before* the effective date of termination.

## Practical Applications

The agent will generate a draft Notice of Termination using the Authors Alliance templates, populating it with data extracted from the USCO Public Catalog. It will generate a strict calendar detailing the exact date by which the notice must be served and recorded.

## Escalation Requirements

Escalate if the current grantee or successor in title cannot be located after a reasonable search, as this requires specialized legal procedures for service by publication or alternative means.
